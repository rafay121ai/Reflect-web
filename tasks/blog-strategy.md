# iReflect — Content / Blog Strategy
_Target: ireflect.app/blog/[slug]_
_ICP: Emotionally exhausted young working women seeking self-understanding_

---

## Strategy Overview

The blog should serve **problem-aware** users who aren't yet searching for an app — they're searching for answers to emotional questions. The content brings them to the page; the product converts them.

**Content tone:** Calm, warm, honest — matches the product. Never clinical, never preachy, never "5 hacks to...". Think: a thoughtful friend who happens to know a lot about self-awareness.

**Publication cadence:** 1 post per 2 weeks to start. Depth over volume.

## Content Format Guidelines

Each post should:
- Be 800–1,200 words (enough for a featured snippet but not padded)
- Open with the emotional problem, not a definition
- Include 2–3 self-reflection questions the reader can try themselves
- End with a soft CTA: "If you want guided self-reflection, iReflect gives you a quiet space to try."
- Never moralize or prescribe — suggest, reflect, invite

## Internal Linking Plan

Each blog post should link:
- Back to the homepage (ireflect.app) with anchor text matching the primary keyword
- To 1–2 other relevant blog posts for topical authority
- The homepage should eventually link to the blog index at ireflect.app/blog

## Featured Snippet Strategy

Posts 1, 3, 4, 6, and 9 are the strongest featured-snippet candidates. Format them with:
- A clear definition or answer in the first 50 words
- H2 subheadings that match follow-up search questions
- A short numbered or bulleted list within the first 300 words
